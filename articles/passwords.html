Passwords

Passwords are the most widely used authentication method on computers and the Internet.

Therefore, we should protect them.

Your passwords should be both secure and memorable.

Make a strong password

Hackers usually will try to guess passwords or use "brute-force" attacks. Brute-force attacks use a program that guess every single possible password until it succeeds. Sometimes hackers will use dictionaries of words to speed up the process. The table on the right shows approximately how long it would take a hacker to brute-force a password according to it's length and variety of characters. That is why an ideal password would be long (8+ characters) and use all available characters (lowercase, uppercase, and punctuation).

Essentials

Make sure your password follows these basic criteria:

- It must be at least 8 characters long.
- It must not contain easily guessed information such your birth date, phone number, spouse’s name, pet’s name, kid’s name, login name, etc.
- It shouldn’t contain words found in the dictionary.
- It should contain special characters such as @#$%^& and/or numbers.
- It should use a variation of upper and lower case letters

Method 1

Turn a sentence into a password

Examples:
WOO!TPwontSB = Woohoo! The Packers won the Super Bowl!
PPupmoarT@O@tgs = Please pick up more Toasty O's at the grocery store.
1tubuupshhh…imj = I tuck button-up shirts into my jeans.
W?ow?imp::ohth3r = Where oh where is my pear? Oh, there.

Method 2

Use a passphrase

Come up with 12 random words
Do not use a simple or well-known phrase from existing literature!

Example: Pantry duck cotton ballcap tissue airplane snore oar Christmas puddle log charism

Using unique passwords, avoiding phishing sites, and keeping your computer safe from password-capturing malware is also important. Yes, you should choose a strong password — but you need to do more than that. Using stronger passwords won’t keep you secure from all the threats out there, but it’s a good first step.
